What is foraging?

Foraging, an age-old practice deeply rooted in human history and shared with various animal species, encompasses the exploration and gathering of food from the natural environment. Historically essential for the survival of early human communities, foraging involved activities like hunting, fishing, and harvesting wild plants and fruits as a means of securing sustenance.

Throughout the course of evolution, foraging has persisted as a fundamental aspect of our relationship with nature. In contemporary times, it has expanded beyond mere survival, taking on new dimensions and applications. Modern foraging may include engaging in the search for mushrooms, uncovering hidden gems in the forest, or selectively gathering herbs with culinary or medicinal value.
